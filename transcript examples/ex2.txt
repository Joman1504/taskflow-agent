Client Call Transcript – Residential Construction Consultation

Participants:
Mr. Carter (Client), Jenna (Project Manager), Luis (Construction Lead), Rachel (Architect), Tom (Estimator)

Jenna: Good afternoon, Mr. Carter. Thanks for joining the call. We understand you’re interested in a home expansion project. Could you walk us through what you have in mind?

Mr. Carter: Sure. I’m looking to add a two-car garage and possibly extend the kitchen area. I also want to include more natural lighting—maybe larger windows or a skylight.

Rachel: That sounds feasible. For the kitchen extension, are you thinking of an open floor plan or something more segmented?

Mr. Carter: Definitely open. I want it to connect better with the living room.

Luis: From a construction standpoint, we’ll need to evaluate the current structure and foundation before confirming feasibility. We may need to reinforce parts of the existing structure.

Tom: I’ll also need to gather some measurements and details so we can provide a cost estimate. Do you have a target budget in mind?

Mr. Carter: I’d like to keep it around $80,000, but I’m flexible if needed.

Jenna: That helps. We can propose options within that range and outline any trade-offs.

Rachel: For the natural lighting, a skylight in the kitchen could work well, and we can also explore expanding the rear-facing windows.

Mr. Carter: That sounds good. I’d prefer something energy-efficient as well.

Luis: We can incorporate energy-efficient materials and insulation. That may slightly increase upfront cost but will save on utilities long-term.

Jenna: As next steps, we’ll schedule a site visit to assess your property.

Mr. Carter: When would that be?

Jenna: We’re available this Thursday or Friday morning.

Mr. Carter: Friday morning works.

Jenna: Great, we’ll confirm for Friday at 9 AM.

Tom: After the visit, I’ll prepare a detailed estimate within about five business days.

Rachel: I’ll also draft preliminary design concepts for your review by early next week.

Mr. Carter: Perfect. How long would construction take?

Luis: For a project of this scope, approximately 6 to 8 weeks, depending on any structural complications.

Jenna: We’ll provide a more precise timeline after the site evaluation.

Mr. Carter: Sounds good. I appreciate the clarity.

Jenna: Thank you, Mr. Carter. We’ll follow up with confirmation details shortly.
